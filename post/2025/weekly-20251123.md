### Faster Sumchecks: Part I
This post explores optimizations for the sumcheck protocol when proving values in a small field with randomness from a large field. By minimizing expensive large × large multiplications, using evaluation tables, precomputing accumulators, and applying Lagrange interpolation, the authors significantly reduce prover time. SageMath implementations are provided for each algorithm.


本文优化了在小域取值、大域取随机数的 sumcheck 协议，通过减少大数乘法、预计算累加器、使用拉格朗日插值等方法显著提升证明效率，并提供了每种算法的 SageMath 实现。

### Lattices, Folding, & Symphony with Binyi Chen
In this episode Anna Rose and Nico Mohnblatt chat with Binyi Chen, researcher at Stanford University. They discuss his work on lattice-based folding schemes, revisit LatticeFold and LatticeFold+, and cover how lattices enable low-cost, post-quantum-secure folding by replacing Pedersen hashes with Ajtai commitments. They discuss the early folding work from 2023 and how it has evolved and explore the advantages of lattices over other approaches in the folding context while also highlighting their tradeoffs.

Binyi goes on to introduce Symphony, his new work that eliminates the need to implement Fiat-Shamir in the recursive verification circuit, and describes how that improves efficiency and removes the chances for a KRS-style attack.

在本期节目中，Anna Rose 和 Nico Mohnblatt 与斯坦福大学的研究员陈斌义进行了对话。他们探讨了陈斌义在基于格的 Folding 方案方面的工作，回顾了 LatticeFold 和 LatticeFold+，并介绍了格如何通过用 Ajtai 承诺替换 Pedersen 哈希来实现低成本、后量子安全的折叠。他们讨论了 2023 年的早期 Folding 工作及其发展历程，并探讨了格在 Folding 领域相对于其他方法的优势，同时也强调了它们的权衡取舍。

陈斌义随后介绍了他的最新成果 Symphony，该方案无需在递归验证电路中实现 Fiat-Shamir 算法，并阐述了这如何提高效率并消除 KRS 式攻击的可能性。
